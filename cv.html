01 / Employment

A track record across academia, industry, and entrepreneurship.

  1. Sep 2023 — Present

    Senior Research Associate

    Institute for Manufacturing (IfM), Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge, United Kingdom

    Specialist in digital systems, condition monitoring, and IoT / Industrial IoT. Leading the Digital Hospitals project as Project Lead and Digital Solution Architect. Developed a modular, service-oriented, autonomous digital twin infrastructure with Cambridge University Hospitals and Addenbrooke's, aimed at operational efficiency in the histopathology department.

    • Founder & Organising Chair, LoDiSA '23, '24, '25 — established a global forum on low-cost digital solutions for industrial automation; now attracts researchers from 20+ universities across 8 countries.
  2. Aug 2023 — Present

    Digital Solutions Architect

    Cambridge University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust, Cambridge, United Kingdom

    Leading research on a service-oriented, modular digital twin platform for hospitals and trialling various aspects of the twin — discrete-event simulation of clinical processes, low-cost digital solutions, and BIM systems. Also researching how low-cost digital monitoring solutions developed for manufacturing can be repurposed for non-clinical healthcare processes.

  3. Jul 2020 — Aug 2023

    Research Associate

    Institute for Manufacturing (IfM), Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge, United Kingdom

    Led research and development of low-cost digitalisation, automation, and analytics solutions for SMEs in the UK and Australia under the EPSRC-funded Digital Manufacturing on a Shoestring project. Developed sensor-based systems (vibration, temperature, current, light) and pioneered testing methodologies for low-cost technologies. Extended the framework to non-patient-centric healthcare to support rapid digitalisation. Collaborated with industry on knowledge discovery and ML-based anomaly detection, and built IoT-enabled monitoring prototypes for hospital equipment. Co-organised hackathons, apprentice training, and international academic workshops.

  4. Sep 2018 — Present

    Co-Founder & Director

    Sensordrops Networks Pvt. Ltd. · Incubated at IIT Kharagpur, India

    Co-founded a company to commercialise IoT solutions developed during doctoral and research projects. Sensordrops delivers integrated IoT-based sensing, monitoring, and control systems for healthcare, industrial, and agricultural applications. Led end-to-end development of all IoT solutions, with successful deployments across large and medium-sized organisations through consultancy projects.

  5. Oct 2014 — Apr 2020

    Senior Research Fellow

    Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India

    Began in the Agriculture and Food Engineering Department at IIT Kharagpur, developing sensor networks for monitoring climate, soil, and water conditions. Contributed to projects on precision robotic systems, underwater sensor networks, blockchain-secured IoT healthcare systems, and IoT-based mine digitalisation. Led project planning, budgeting, equipment procurement, team coordination, and interaction with funding bodies. Co-developed and delivered 12+ industry- and academia-facing workshops on IoT, sensor networks, UAVs, and underwater systems.

    As a Ph.D. scholar, focused on constrained IoT networks and edge computing, with research emphasising adaptive task offloading in resource-limited environments. Developed intelligent, scalable, and interoperable networked systems for agriculture, healthcare, and industrial monitoring — addressing mobility, energy efficiency, and sustainability challenges via networking and machine-learning approaches. Also engaged in teaching and mentoring during the doctoral tenure.

  6. Jun 2012 — Oct 2014

    Assistant Professor

    Dept. of Electronics & Communication Engineering, University of Engineering & Management, Jaipur, India

    Served in the newly established Department of Electronics and Communications Engineering, where I developed and taught 6+ undergraduate courses spanning electronics, microelectronics, electrical engineering, signal processing, data communications, and solid-state devices — covering both theory and lab components.

    • Acting Head of Department for two years — streamlined academic and administrative operations and laid the groundwork for long-term departmental growth.
    • Established seven new laboratories to enhance research and teaching infrastructure.
    • Organised student-focused workshops, internships, industrial visits, and technical exhibitions to promote hands-on learning and industry exposure.
