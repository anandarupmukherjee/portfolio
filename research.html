Research

Research & Projects

Designing modular, low-cost, resilient architectures for organisations and systems fragmented by information silos.

Theme

Rewiring the digital fabric — from fragmentation to democratised transformation.

My research explores the structure and flow of information within complex systems — from distributed assets and technical platforms to organisational processes. I study how information silos emerge, persist, and impact decision-making, and design solutions that promote resilience, interoperability, and intelligent autonomy. Through distributed computing, modular platform design, and human-system integration, my goal is to create adaptive digital infrastructures that scale affordably and operate robustly, even in resource-constrained or high-uncertainty environments.

  • Platform Ecosystems

    Digital twins and IoT integration frameworks for hospitals, factories, and logistics.

  • Networks of Assets

    UAV swarms, industrial plant floors, and healthcare infrastructure — coordinated at the edge.

  • Organisations as Systems

    SMEs, hospitals, and logistics providers treated as adaptive, fragmented, constrained systems.

  • Democratised Digitalisation

    Low-cost, modular, resilient architectures that scale to resource-constrained contexts.

University of Cambridge (2020 — Present)

Projects spanning healthcare digital twins, low-cost industrial digitalisation, port logistics, telecoms network resilience, and SME digital adoption across the UK, Australia, and India.

Active2023 — Present

Digital Hospitals

Leading the development of a service-oriented, modular digital twin platform for hospitals, trialed with the Histopathology department at Addenbrooke's. Integrates discrete-event simulation of clinical processes, low-cost digital solutions, and BIM systems. Advises multiple NHS bodies, clinical consultants, and medical supply-chain partners.

Funded by · Cambridge University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust
Active2024 — Present

Shoestring Digital Manufacturing — India

Establishing engagement pipelines for Indian manufacturing SMEs to understand, prioritise, and adopt digital solutions. Over 60 companies engaged in Jan 2025; pilot digital solutions currently deployed across four SMEs in two regions. Partners include IIT Indore, IITI DRISHTI CPS Foundation (TIH), and the Mahindra Institute of Quality.

Partners · IIT Indore · DRISHTI CPS · MIQ
OngoingEPSRC

Digital Manufacturing on a Shoestring

Piloted low-cost, modular digitalisation approaches for UK and Scottish manufacturing SMEs. Proposed a novel testing methodology for low-cost COTS systems, co-evolved an impact assessment framework for pre- and post-deployment evaluation, and developed a modular energy analytics framework for manufacturing industries.

Funded by · EPSRC
CompletedBoeing

LoCOST — Low-cost Automation using Off-the-Shelf Technologies

Architected low-cost monitoring solutions for Boeing's manufacturing plant in Sheffield, UK. Preserved existing major technologies while designing flexibility into edge-case scenarios as they arose, grounding the research in concrete factual requirements rather than assumptions.

Funded by · Boeing
Completed2021 — 2022

5G Ports — Predictive Maintenance at Felixstowe

Collaborated with industry partners to test the potential of 5G at the Port of Felixstowe. Investigated and developed a predictive maintenance approach for live multimodal sensor signatures from PLCs and IoT sensors, and built a constrained discord detection mechanism using crane motor vibration signatures.

Partner · Port of Felixstowe · 5G Ports programme
CompletedUKWHA

Logistics on a Shoestring

Architected low-cost digital approaches for enhancing operational efficiency across logistics companies and warehouse operations — including electric vehicle monitoring within warehouses, asset tracking, and inter-site movement analytics.

Funded by · UK Warehouse Association (UKWHA)
CompletedUKRI · BT

NG-CDI — Next Generation Converged Digital Infrastructures

Investigated and architected approaches for anomaly detection across British Telecom's broadband network infrastructure. Developed a dynamic anomaly detection approach for localising faults and fault severity in individual network links.

Funded by · UKRI & British Telecom
CompletedDPIRD · Australia

Shoestring Digital Manufacturing — Western Australia

Architected low-cost digital monitoring solutions and investigated approaches for remote deployment across various Western Australian business associations, including WA Corngrowers, Brewers Association, Wine Makers Association, and other regional entities.

Funded by · DPIRD, Government of Australia
OngoingInnovate UK

Smart Manufacturing Data Hub (SMDH)

Architected standalone low-cost digital monitoring solutions and systems interfacing with industrial machines. Solutions are being deployed across Northern Ireland, Wales, and England with the broader project team in over 70+ SMEs.

Funded by · Innovate UK
02 / IIT Kharagpur

Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur (2014 — 2020)

Doctoral and post-doctoral work on IoT for constrained networks, UAV swarms, agricultural sensing, blockchain-secured healthcare, and digital mines.

CompletedMeitY, GoI

Development of Digital Mines using IoT

Developed an integrated IoT monitoring system for underground coal mines — hazardous gas detection, strata monitoring and strata-fall prediction, mine fire detection, and tracking of temperature, pressure, and humidity. Contributions spanned algorithms, methods, and hardware for worker safety in harsh subterranean conditions.

Funded by · Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology, Government of India
CompletedUKIERI

SAFE — Safe and Usable IoT Ecosystem

Indo-UK project investigating algorithms, methods, and hardware for acquiring and encrypting healthcare data, then releasing it securely onto an IoT-based blockchain. Collaboration between IIT Kharagpur and UK partners.

Funded by · UGC-UKIERI (India–UK Joint Research Initiative)
CompletedICAR

Development of Precision Robotic Planter

Designed a live-scale precision planter that autonomously navigates crop rows to plant seedlings at pre-defined intervals with fine spacing and depth control. Conceptualised and implemented vision and non-vision positioning and navigation algorithms and hardware for the agricultural robotic planter.

Funded by · Indian Council for Agricultural Research (ICAR), Government of India
CompletedITRA · MeitY

Water-Use Efficiency via Sensor Networks

Developed an experimental sensor network for monitoring climate, soil (nutrient and fertiliser), and water conditions during crop-growing seasons at field scale. Built an IoT and cloud-based infrastructure for integrating field data and designed low-cost smartphone apps and sensor nodes for in-field parameter monitoring, feeding into a hydrologic modelling framework at watershed scale.

Funded by · Information Technology Research Academy, Media Lab Asia, DeitY
